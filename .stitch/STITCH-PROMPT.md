## Prompt

A premium cybersecurity consulting firm single-page website. The brand is "CCyber" (Convergent Cyber Solutions). The aesthetic is "Architectural Oversight" — elite corporate advisory, NOT hacker/neon/cyberpunk. Think McKinsey meets cybersecurity.

**BRAND IDENTITY:**
The logo is a blue shield outline with purple double-chevron arrows inside it, next to "CCYBER" in heavy steel-gray type. Derive the entire color palette from this logo:
- Steel Blue (#4a7ab5) — primary brand color, shield outline
- Muted Lavender (#7b6b9e) — accent, chevron arrows
- Dark Navy (#161c2e) — background base
- Steel Gray (#5a6672) — body text, secondary elements
- Light Steel (#c8d0d8) — headlines, primary text

**DESIGN SYSTEM (REQUIRED):**
- Platform: Web, Desktop-first, single page with scroll sections
- Color Mode: Dark — deep navy-charcoal foundation, never pure black
- Typography: Manrope throughout — Display 3.5rem tight tracking for hero, 2rem section headlines, 1rem body with 1.6 line-height, 0.75rem uppercase labels with wide tracking
- Corner Radius: 4px for buttons and cards (sharp, architectural). Pill shape (rounded-full) ONLY for small tags/chips
- Borders: NO 1px solid borders for section dividers. Use background color shifts between surface tiers for separation
- Shadows: Ambient only — 0px 24px 48px rgba(0,0,0,0.4) for floating elements. No harsh drop shadows
- Glassmorphism: Floating nav bar with backdrop-blur 16px, semi-transparent surface at 40% opacity
- Background texture: Faint geometric node-grid (1px dots at 40px intervals, 5% opacity) in hero section

**PAGE STRUCTURE:**

1. **Navigation Bar** — Floating, full-width glassmorphism bar. Left: Logo (blue shield + "CONVERGENT" bold tracked / "CYBER SOLUTIONS" smaller tracked below). Center: nav links "Solutions · Services · About Us · Resources" in muted text. Right: Pill-outlined "Contact" button with border.

2. **Hero Section** — Full viewport height. Left side: large bold headline "Securing Your Digital Future" in light steel, subtext "Advanced protection for enterprises in a rapidly evolving threat landscape." in muted gray. Two pill-shaped CTA buttons: "Get Started" (solid muted fill with backdrop blur) and "Learn More" (outlined). Right-center: Large 3D faceted glass shield — frosted translucent gray panels with subtle steel-blue edge reflections, overlapping partially with the text area. Behind the shield: geometric polygon faceted background covering the right 60% of the viewport — angular triangular panels in varying dark grays (#2a2f33 to #3a3f43) with visible edge lines.

3. **Services Section** — Background shifts to slightly lighter surface tier. Section label "Our Expertise" in uppercase muted text. Headline "Architectural Cybersecurity For The Modern Enterprise". Three service cards side by side on elevated surface — no borders, separated by background tone. Cards: (a) "Strategic Advisory" — peer-level guidance for cybersecurity firms, (b) "Virtual CISO" — executive security leadership on demand, (c) "AI-Driven GRC" — governance, risk & compliance with CMMC, NIST 800-171, FedRAMP. Each card has an icon, title, description, and "Learn More" link with arrow.

4. **Why Convergent Section** — Asymmetric two-column layout. Left: headline "Built Different. On Purpose." with subtext about boutique precision. Right: three stacked cards — "Boutique Precision", "AI-Native Tooling", "Federal Depth" — each with description text on recessed surface background.

5. **CTA Section** — Centered. "Ready to fortify your organizational architecture?" headline. Single gradient-accent button "Initiate Strategic Briefing".

6. **Footer** — Minimal. Company name left, email and copyright right. Subtle surface shift, no border.

**ATMOSPHERE:**
Corporate gravitas. Muted confidence. Every element has breathing room — generous whitespace. The design should feel like walking into the lobby of a high-end security operations center: quiet, controlled, architecturally precise. The shield is the hero visual — it should command attention as a symbol of protection and convergence.
